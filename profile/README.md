<h1>Mail Merge Toolkit - Overview</h1>

<h2>Purpose of the program</h2>
<p><strong>Mail Merge Toolkit</strong> is a Microsoft Office add‑in designed to expand and enhance the standard mail‑merge capabilities in Word, Publisher and Outlook. It gives users the ability to perform more flexible and feature‑rich mail merges - not just generating personalized letters, but sending mass emails with individual attachments, dynamic subjects, and in various formats directly via Outlook. The tool is developed by MAPILab Ltd..</p>

<h2>Main features</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Personalized email subject lines</strong> - you can insert merge‑fields (e.g. recipient's name) into the subject field so every message feels custom.</li>
  <li><strong>Attachments support</strong> - attach one or more files to each message. Attachments may be the same for all recipients or selected individually per recipient.</li>
  <li><strong>Flexible message formats</strong> - send mailings in HTML, RTF, GIF or plain‑text formats directly from Word, Outlook or Publisher.</li>
  <li><strong>Merge to email via Outlook</strong> - instead of printing letters or generating documents, Mail Merge Toolkit sends the personalized messages through Outlook, making bulk emailing seamless.</li>
  <li><strong>Password‑protected PDF/DOCX attachments</strong> - you can protect sensitive documents before sending, enhancing security when distributing private data.</li>
  <li><strong>Multiple recipients and CC/BCC support</strong> - allows sending to multiple addresses per record, including CC or BCC recipients.</li>
  <li><strong>Compatibility with many Office versions</strong> - works with a wide range of Microsoft Office releases, including Office 2003, 2007, 2010, 2013, 2016, and 365 editions.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Unique aspects</h2>
<p>Standard mail‑merge tools bundled with Office allow merging document content (like letters or labels) with data sources, but they don't support sending personalized emails with attachments or customizing subject lines. Mail Merge Toolkit fills this gap - enabling a full-featured mass‑mailing workflow inside Office without needing external email‑marketing services. Its tight integration with Outlook and flexibility (format, attachments, personalization) make it especially useful for business correspondence, newsletters, invoices, or any scenario needing many customized emails.</p>

<h2>Target audience</h2>
<p>This add‑in is aimed at small businesses, marketing teams, nonprofits, educational institutions, and any users who need to send bulk but personalized emails - for newsletters, invoices, notifications, announcements, invitations - directly from their desktop Office environment. Also useful for administrators and managers who prefer to keep mailings internal (no external mailing services) and maintain data privacy.</p>

<h2>Advantages of using Mail Merge Toolkit</h2>

  <li>Allows full mail‑merge emailing with attachments, something standard Office lacks. </li>
  <li>Enables more professional and personalized bulk communication (custom subject, attachments, secure PDFs). </li>
  <li>Runs locally - no external servers; sensitive data (recipient info, attachments) stays on your computer.</li>
  <li>Compatible with many versions of Windows and Office - suitable even for older systems.</li>

<h2>Functional modules & workflow</h2>
<p>The typical workflow with Mail Merge Toolkit looks like this:</p>
<ol>
  <li>Prepare a mail template in Microsoft Word, Publisher or Outlook (body text, placeholders for merge fields, optional attachments or placeholders).</li>
  <li>Prepare the data source - Excel spreadsheet, CSV, or other supported database/contact list containing recipients data.</li>
  <li>Start mail merge: select recipients list, map data fields (e.g. name, email, custom fields), set subject with placeholders, choose attachments and format (HTML, RTF, etc.).</li>
  <li>The add-in processes records: for each row in data source, it generates a unique message - replacing placeholders, adding attachments, customizing subject/body - then sends it via Outlook (or saves to Draft folder).</li>
  <li>Optionally review or delay sending, send in batches, manage multiple accounts or send from different Outlook profiles.</li>
</ol>
